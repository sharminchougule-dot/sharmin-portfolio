About Me

Sharmin N. Chougule is a Research Assistant at the Institut für das Recht der Digitalisierung (IRDi), University of Marburg, Germany. She holds the Doctor Europaeus title in Civil Law and Constitutional Legality from the International School of Advanced Studies, University of Camerino, Italy.

Her research focuses on novel legal issues of the Internet of Things and Distributed Ledger Technology in Smart Contracts, as well as legal issues of AI contracting, comprehending the convergence of technology and law. She brings enriching experience as an in-house Contracts Counsel with Integreon, providing legal support to a global sportswear company in the United States, White Bison (Zürich), and Publicis Groupe, India Country Office.

Sharmin is a Member of the Academic Advisory Body at INATBA - International Association for Trusted Blockchain Applications and a Corporate Compliance Officer under the Institute of Company Secretaries of India. She holds an LL.M. degree in International Law (with Distinction) from the Indian Law Society (ILS) Law College, University of Pune, India.

The Oracle Problem in Smart Contracts: Data Privacy, Security, and Solutions

Co-authored with Luigi Cantisani [2024]. Explores risks associated with the Oracle problem in smart contracts, including data privacy risks, data manipulation risks, trust in Oracle operators, and regulatory compliance concerns. Discusses solutions such as privacy-preserving oracles, decentralized oracle networks, and reputation-based models.

Smart Contracts Blockchain Data Privacy

Contemplating Basic Global Regulatory Framework to Safeguard Personal Data

Part of LL.M. thesis [2023]. Focused on the proposition that despite substantial departure in various detailed personal data protection laws globally, there remains better consensus at the core of most national laws and international regimes upon certain set of personal data protection principles.
